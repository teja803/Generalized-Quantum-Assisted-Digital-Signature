How Quantum Randomness Strengthens Signatures

🌀 Quantum Superposition

Quantum bits (qubits) exist in multiple states simultaneously until measured, providing inherent unpredictability.

📊 True Randomness

Unlike pseudo-random generators, quantum randomness cannot be predicted or reproduced, even with unlimited computational power.

🛡️ Enhanced Security

Quantum seeds make signatures resistant to attacks that exploit patterns in traditional random number generators.

🔮 Future-Proof

Even quantum computers cannot predict the output of quantum random number generators.

Glossary

Digital Signature

A mathematical scheme for verifying the authenticity and integrity of digital messages or documents.

Hash Function

A one-way cryptographic function that converts input data of any size into a fixed-size output (digest).

Quantum Random Number Generator (QRNG)

A device that generates truly random numbers using quantum mechanical phenomena like photon detection.

Public Key

A cryptographic key that can be shared publicly and is used to verify signatures or encrypt data.

Private Key

A secret cryptographic key that must be kept confidential and is used to create signatures or decrypt data.

Post-Quantum Cryptography

Cryptographic algorithms designed to be secure against attacks by both classical and quantum computers.

Entropy

A measure of randomness or unpredictability in a system. High entropy means more randomness.

Non-repudiation

The assurance that someone cannot deny the validity of their signature on a document.
